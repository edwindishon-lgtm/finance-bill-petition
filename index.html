🚨 Reject Finance Bill 2026

Send your objection during public participation.

Key Concerns:

• Agency notices during disputes
• Higher tax burden on businesses
• Digital payment taxes
• Mobile phone excise duty

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing to formally express my strong reservations and rejection of the Finance Bill 2026 in its current form. While I acknowledge the government’s intention to enhance revenue collection and improve tax compliance, several provisions in the Bill raise serious economic, legal, and social concerns that outweigh its anticipated benefits.

1. Expansion of Agency Notices During Ongoing Tax Disputes

The proposal allowing KRA to issue agency notices and freeze or transfer funds even while a taxpayer’s appeal is pending undermines fairness and due process. Businesses may suffer cash flow disruptions even before disputes are resolved.

2. Reduced Time for Filing Tax Returns

Shortened filing deadlines reduce preparation time, increasing errors and penalties, especially for SMEs with limited accounting capacity.

3. Taxation of Undistributed Profits

Taxing retained earnings discourages reinvestment, job creation, and business growth.

4. VAT Invoice Expansion

Extending VAT invoice requirements increases compliance burdens for small and informal businesses.

5. Digital Payment Taxes

Higher taxes on digital transactions increase costs and discourage digital economy growth.

6. Mobile Phone Excise Duty

The 25% excise duty increases the cost of digital access and widens the digital divide.

Conclusion

The Finance Bill 2026 risks increasing cost of living, discouraging investment, and overburdening businesses. I therefore urge that it be rejected or substantially revised.
