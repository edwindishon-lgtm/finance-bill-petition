🚨 Reject Finance Bill 2026

To: Parliament of Kenya – Public Participation Committee

Formal Public Objection Submission

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing as a concerned Kenyan citizen to formally reject the Finance Bill 2026 due to its negative impact on the economy, businesses, and ordinary citizens.

Key Concerns

  • 25% excise duty on mobile phones increasing cost of digital access
  • Higher taxes on digital payments and fintech services
  • Agency notices during ongoing tax disputes
  • Taxation of undistributed company profits
  • Reduced time for filing tax returns
  • Higher withholding tax on EAC dividends
  • Expanded VAT invoice requirements
  • Stricter virtual asset reporting rules

Conclusion

While some exemptions (like solar and medical goods) are positive, the overall impact of the Finance Bill 2026 is harmful to affordability, investment, and economic growth.

I therefore urge Parliament to reject or substantially revise the Bill.
