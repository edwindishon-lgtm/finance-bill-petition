🚨 Reject Finance Bill 2026

To: Clerk of Parliament & Committee on Finance Bill 2026

Formal Public Objection Submission

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing as a concerned Kenyan citizen to formally reject the Finance Bill 2026 due to its negative economic, social, and business impacts. While revenue collection is important, several proposals place excessive burden on citizens and businesses.

Key Concerns

  • 25% excise duty on mobile phones increasing cost of digital access
  • Higher taxes on digital payments and fintech services
  • Agency notices during ongoing tax disputes
  • Taxation of undistributed company profits
  • Reduced time for filing tax returns
  • Higher withholding tax on EAC dividends
  • Expanded VAT invoice requirements
  • Stricter crypto and virtual asset reporting rules

Conclusion

While some exemptions such as solar and medical goods are positive, the overall impact of the Finance Bill 2026 is harmful to economic growth, investment, and affordability of essential services.

I therefore respectfully urge Parliament to reject or substantially revise the Bill.
