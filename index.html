🚨 Reject Finance Bill 2026

Public Participation Submission

Formal Objection

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing as a concerned Kenyan citizen to formally reject the Finance Bill 2026 due to its negative economic impact on citizens, businesses, and the digital economy.

While revenue collection is necessary, several proposals impose excessive financial pressure on already struggling households and businesses.

Key Concerns

  • 25% excise duty on mobile phones increasing cost of digital access
  • Higher taxes on digital payments and fintech services
  • Agency notices during ongoing tax disputes
  • Taxation of undistributed company profits
  • Reduced tax filing timelines
  • Higher withholding tax on EAC dividends
  • Expanded VAT invoice requirements
  • Virtual asset reporting restrictions

Conclusion

Although VAT exemptions on essential goods like solar and medical supplies are positive, the overall impact of the Finance Bill 2026 is harmful to affordability, investment, and economic growth.

I therefore urge Parliament to reject or substantially revise the Bill.
